What is a zero-interest credit card?

The different types of credit card APR


APR is short for annual percentage rate and is the rate youâ€™ll pay in interest if you carry a balance on your credit card. If you want to know more about how credit card APRs work, one key thing to note is not all APRs are the same.

Regular or ongoing APR is the standard interest rate issued to you for purchases with the credit card, when you carry a balance. The regular APR is the interest rate charged for purchases if there is no promotional APR offer.



Purchase APR

Balance transfer APR


A balance transfer APR is the interest rate applied when you transfer an existing balance from one credit card to another. A card may offer either a zero percent purchase APR or zero percent balance transfer APR, but not both. A cash advance APR is the interest rate applied when you use a credit card for a cash advance. Itâ€™s important to note the interest on a cash advance begins immediately accruing the day you receive the cash advance, and not at the end of a grace period the way a purchase APR does.
