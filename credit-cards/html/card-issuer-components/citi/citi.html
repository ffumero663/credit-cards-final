Best for cash back + sign-up bonus: Citi Custom Cash® Card

  • Best features: This no annual fee card gets you 5% cash back on purchases for the first $500 spent in your top eligible spend category each billing cycle (then 1%). The 5% rate automatically adjusts to your spending rather than the task of sorting out rotating spend categories on your own. Your purchases outside of the top spend category will earn 1% cash back. New cardholders can also earn $200 in cash back (paid out in ThankYou points) after spending $1,500 in the first six months of account opening. There's also a 15-month 0% intro APR on balance transfers and purchases (18.74% - 28.74% Variable APR afterwards).
  • Biggest drawbacks: The $500 cap on your bonus rate each billing cycle is less than ideal, totaling just $6,000 in annual spending. While the card’s intro APR offer on balance transfers is great, there’s a $5 or 5% transfer fee on any balance moved. Also, there’s no annual bonuses or high-rate offers outside of your top category.
  • Alternatives: Those looking for a flexible cash back card with similar options should look to the Bank of America® Customized Cash Rewards credit card’s offer of 3% cash back in the category of your choice and 2% back at wholesale clubs and grocery stores (on up to $2,500 in combined choice category/grocery store/wholesale club quarterly purchases, then 1%).
  • Bottom line: Shoppers with spending habits that vary month to month can really benefit from this card’s user-friendly rewards structure. Consistent 5% cash back is a great rate for any credit card, especially one with no annual fee.

Best for cash back: Citi Double Cash® Card

  • Best features: Cardholders who stay on top of their payments get an unlimited 2% cash back on every purchase, a huge earnings opportunity for big spenders. To incentivize good habits, it’s 1% cash back on every purchase (excluding gift cards), plus an additional 1% as you pay off those purchases. Your cash back rewards can be converted to ThankYou points, which can be redeemed for travel purchases, gift cards and more for a rate of $1 cash back to 100 ThankYou points. Additionally, this no annual fee card offers an introductory 18-month 0% APR on balance transfers (18.74% - 28.74% Variable APR thereafter).
  • Biggest drawbacks: Your full rewards rate depends on you making sufficient payments.
  • Alternatives: Someone on the hunt for consistent 2% cash rewards might prefer the Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card. Earn 2% cash rewards on purchases and take advantage of an introductory offer of $200 cash rewards bonus after you spend at least $500 in purchases in your first three months.
  • Bottom line: Unlimited 2% cash back can benefit nearly anyone as an everyday card, but it does require a certain level of financial discipline to reap the full benefits. If you can stay on top of payments, the lack of complicated rewards and a strong balance transfer offer make this a great choice for someone looking for a straightforward option or a chance at tackling their debt.

What is Citi?

Citibank, a division of Citigroup, is a full-service bank that provides mortgages, checking and savings accounts, credit cards, and more.

One of North America’s oldest banks in continuous operation, Citibank is steeped in the United States’ banking history. It was founded in 1812 as City Bank of New York, with Samuel Osgood as the founding president. In 1968, Citibank introduced its first credit card, known as the Everything Card, later partnering with Mastercard. In the 1970s, Citibank was one of the first American banks to introduce automatic teller machines to its customers.

Today, Citibank has 2,649 branches throughout the world, 723 of which are located in the United States. Citibank’s U.S. presence is focused primarily in the metropolitan areas around New York, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago, Washington D.C., and Miami. As Citi has expanded to new locations over the years, the bank’s selection of credit cards has grown just as significantly, now providing options for nearly any type of consumer.

How to choose a Citi credit card

Using tools like our CardMatch™ feature can help you get an idea of what you could qualify for based on your credit profile and make it easier to sort through your options. You can even see what Citi cards you may be prequalified for before making any choices.

Your budget, where you spend and what you do in your everyday life all matter when finding the right card. Take a second to consider what you need most out of a credit card – it’ll make your application process simpler from there.

Debt relief

Citi has several options with long introductory APR periods, perfect for consolidating outstanding credit card debt to pay off while temporarily avoiding interest. The Citi Simplicity and Diamond Preferred cards each bring a generous introductory APR period, and those who don’t want to lose out on rewards while tackling debt could look at the Citi Rewards+ or Citi Custom Cash’s intro APR offers.

Well-rounded cash back

The unique offer of the Citi Custom Cash makes it a very strong cash back option for any shopper, especially those with a particularly high spending category. The Citi Double Cash also brings plenty to the plate with it’s 2% effective cash back rate on all purchases (1% when you buy and 1% as you pay).

Airline miles

Citi offers several high-value airline cards for American Airline loyalists. Those who want the elite travel experience should consider the slew of perks that come with the Citi/AAdvantage Executive World Elite Mastercard, but those who are getting started with earning flight rewards might see more appeal in the less expensive American Airlines AAdvantage MileUp.

Gas rewards

You’ll have to be at least interested in being a Costco member to take advantage here, but everyday credit card users and small business owners who have an especially high budget on gas or electric vehicle charging will love the 4% cash back (on up to $7,000 worth at the pump per year, then 1%) brought by the Costco Anywhere Visa Card and the Costco Anywhere Visa Business.

Credit building

Those who are just starting with their credit-building journeys or recovering after a depleted score could benefit from a secured option. The Citi Secured Mastercard is a no-frills card that comes without an annual fee, so you can save on extra costs to make payments that eventually build your score.

What programs does Citi offer?

Citi’s catalog of cards brings a lot to the table. On top of the main attractions like cash back rewards, travel points and debt relief opportunities, Citi’s cards come with some strong additional benefits and user-friendly programs to make them worthwhile. Earning ThankYou rewards and taking advantage of Citi’s other offers might be easy, but it’s always important to make sure the card’s perks and other extras actually apply to your lifestyle and finances; a mismatched card and cardholder can cause problems.

Besides each card’s respective rates and rewards, many Citi credit cards come equipped with useful add-ons, such as standard travel, shopping and security perks like account monitoring, fraud protection and a free concierge service.

We’ve included a list of other Citi credit card benefits, headlined by Citi Easy Deals and the Citi Entertainment® program.

Citi Easy Deals

Cardholders with either the Citi Simplicity or Citi Diamond Preferred can qualify for discounts on restaurants, merchandise and more through Citi Easy Deals. Easy Deals operates according to tiers – depending on certain annual spending requirements, users can climb and access more deals. Although most of the discounts aren’t anything you couldn’t get from online coupons, the meatier travel and gift card deals in the upper tiers could be worth your while.

  • Base tier – Gives you access upon registration to local offers for restaurants, shops and services.
  • Enhanced tier – In addition to Base tier benefits, spend $100 in purchases on your eligible credit card in one calendar year for access to savings on gift cards and magazines.
  • Plus tier – In addition to Base and Enhanced benefits, spend $500 in purchases on your eligible credit card in one calendar year for access to savings on merchandise, travel and Daily Deals.

Citi Entertainment

With Citi Entertainment, qualifying cardholders gain access to reserved pre-sale tickets for events in four categories: music, sports, dining and “theater and more.” Users subscribed to Citi Entertainment’s email list can score first dibs on special deals like LiveNation virtual concert tickets and other livestream events. Although other issuers offer similar experience access programs, like Capital One Access and Chase Experiences, Citi Entertainment comes at no added cost and pretty much every Citi card (even select debit cards) is eligible. Cards with Citi Entertainment will have the Visa, Mastercard or American Express logo on them.
