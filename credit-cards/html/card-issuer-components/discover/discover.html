    What is Disover?

    Discover is best known for its stellar lineup of cash back cards, but it is more than just a card issuer. Discover is also a network, along with Visa, Mastercard and American Express. This means it partners with merchants nationally and worldwide, making Discover cards viable payment across numerous countries.

    Discover offers several more financial services as well, such as online banking, student loans, home loans and personal loans. They also offer their customers useful add-ons, like a free look at your FICO score and identity theft protection. These connections and services have made Discover a top provider of cards for cash back, everyday earnings, small businesses, beginners and more.

    Where is Discover accepted?

    Despite being the smallest major U.S. network, Discover is still widely accepted by 99% of merchants who accept credit cards. It’s also accepted in 200 countries and territories. With widespread acceptance both in the states and around the globe, Discover’s cards can be great travel companions. If you’re planning a trip, you can check the Discover Card International Acceptance Map to see if your card is good-to-go.

    Some of Discover’s cards will require a credit score in the good or excellent range, meaning your score will likely need to be at least 670 for their top choices. That being said, Discover has options for everyone no matter the credit score.

After setting a trend of good habits with one of those starter cards, you can grow your credit score and move on to a Discover card that features better benefits.

    As a top provider for customer satisfaction, Discover gives ways for their cardholders to earn great rewards and benefits. Knowing their featured programs can help you take full advantage of their offers and level-up as a cardholder.

    Discover’s rotating cash back

    Discover’s 2023 Cashback Calendar is available. To ensure you earn the full cash back rate, keep the following in mind:

    • You must activate the bonus each quarter to receive 5% back.
    • Your bonus is active from the first day of the first month of each quarter – or the date on which you activate it, whichever is later – through the last day of the last month of each quarter.
    • You earn bonus cash back up to the quarterly maximum of $1,500 in purchases.
    • All spending over the quarterly maximum and all purchases outside a quarter’s bonus category earn 1% cash back.

    Discover’s reward redemption options

    Since cash back reward values are pretty straightforward, the best cash back redemption options are often the most convenient for you at the moment without sacrificing reward value. For gift card lovers, however, Discover has a great deal: There is an added value on each gift card you redeem for (for example, certain merchants offer a $25 gift card for $20 of cash back rewards).

    When you’re ready to collect your hard-earned Discover credit card rewards, here’s a quick rundown of your Discover redemption options:

    • Cash, either as a direct deposit or a statement credit – 1 cent minimum redemption
    • Amazon.com purchases, using the “Cashback Bonus®” option at checkout ($1 Cashback Bonus = $1) – no minimum redemption value
    • Gift cards (with an added bonus value) – ranging from $5 – $200 in increments of $5 (minimum redemption)
    • Charity donations – 1 cent minimum redemption

    Discover it Miles can be redeemed as a credit toward eligible travel purchases on your credit card statement or as a bank account direct deposit, both at 1 cent per 1 mile. Plus, Discover’s cash back and miles don’t expire. Rather than erasing your rewards, Discover will credit your account with your cash back or miles balance if your account is closed or it hasn’t been used within 18 months.

    What is the average credit limit on a Discover card?
    +

    Discover doesn’t provide information about average credit limits. According to data from Experian, the average credit limit for Americans is $30,365 across all their credit cards. But individual credit limits vary widely.

    A number of Discover cardholders revealed their credit limits on a myFICO forum. Some reported limits as low as $300, while others reported limits that soared into the thousands. These variances show that the average credit limit for a Discover card is less important than knowing the factors that go into determining your individual credit limit. These include income, credit score, and the information contained in your credit report.

    Your credit score can give you a quick idea of a good credit limit. The American Banking Association notes that an average credit limit for someone with a super-prime credit score (759 or higher) is $9,329, while a prime credit score (between 680 to 759) has an average credit limit of $5,109, and subprime scores (680 and below) have an average limit of $2,541.

    What is the minimum payment on a Discover card?
    +

    The smallest total amount you will owe on a Discover card each month depends on your creditworthiness and the terms of your cardmember agreement.

    For Discover cardmembers with excellent credit, the minimum payment due will be any past due balance plus one of the following (whichever is greater):

    • $35
    • 2% of the new balance shown on your billing statement
    • $20, plus any interest charges, late fees or fees for any debt protection product as shown on your billing statement.

    For people working on their credit or who have the Discover it® Secured Credit Card, your minimum payment will at least be $20, but it could be higher.

    According to a sample cardmember agreement for a secured credit card account, if you have a past due balance, you’ll owe that plus the greater of the following: 3% of the new balance (minus interest charges and late fees shown on your billing statement) or $15 plus any interest charges, late fees (not to exceed 4% of the new balance), or fees for any debt protection product.
