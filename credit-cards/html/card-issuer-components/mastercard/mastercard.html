What is Mastercard?

Mastercard is one of the largest electronic payment networks in the world. It processes the transactions that consumers make using credit, debit, or prepaid cards. There are four main payment networks: Visa is the largest based on credit card market share, followed by Mastercard, Discover, and American Express.

Rather than issue cards, Mastercard partners with financial institutions like Capital One and Bank of America, as well as local banks or credit unions. These are the issuers that provide the cards that carry the Mastercard logo. Mastercard also partners with millions of merchants around the world. When you use your card, the merchant is charged a fee to have the payment network quickly relay information between the merchantâ€™s bank and the bank that issued the card.
